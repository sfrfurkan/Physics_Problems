# Task 03 – Electrostatic Equilibrium Between Two Positive Charges

## Problem Statement

A charge $q_3 = +1\,\mathrm{C}$ is placed on the line between two fixed charges $q_1 = +4\,\mathrm{C}$ and $q_2 = +9\,\mathrm{C}$. The distance between $q_1$ and $q_2$ is $2\,\mathrm{m}$. Find the equilibrium position of $q_3$.

## Theory

For equilibrium on a line, the net force on the charge must be zero:

$$
F_1 = F_2
$$

Since all charges are positive, the forces on $q_3$ from $q_1$ and $q_2$ are repulsive. Between the two charges, these repulsions act in opposite directions, so cancellation is possible there.

Using Coulomb's law,

$$
k \frac{q_1 q_3}{x^2} = k \frac{q_2 q_3}{(2-x)^2}
$$

where $x$ is the distance from $q_1$ to the equilibrium point.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Let the equilibrium point be at distance $x$ from $q_1$, so its distance from $q_2$ is

$$
2 - x
$$

Set the magnitudes of the forces equal:

$$
k \frac{4 \cdot 1}{x^2} = k \frac{9 \cdot 1}{(2-x)^2}
$$

Cancel $k$:

$$
\frac{4}{x^2} = \frac{9}{(2-x)^2}
$$

Take square roots:

$$
\frac{2}{x} = \frac{3}{2-x}
$$

Cross-multiply:

$$
2(2-x) = 3x
$$

$$
4 - 2x = 3x
$$

$$
4 = 5x
$$

$$
x = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8\,\mathrm{m}
$$

Thus the equilibrium point is

$$
0.8\,\mathrm{m}
$$

from $q_1$ and

$$
2.0 - 0.8 = 1.2\,\mathrm{m}
$$

from $q_2$.

## Final Result

The charge $q_3$ is in equilibrium at

$$
x = 0.8\,\mathrm{m}
$$

from the $+4\,\mathrm{C}$ charge, or equivalently

$$
1.2\,\mathrm{m}
$$

from the $+9\,\mathrm{C}$ charge.

## Interpretation

The equilibrium point lies closer to the smaller charge. This is expected because the weaker charge must be closer to produce a force equal in magnitude to that from the stronger charge.
