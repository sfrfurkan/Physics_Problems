# Task 08 – Lorentz Force on a Charged Particle

## Problem Statement

A charged particle with

$$
q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

and mass

$$
m = 4 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

enters a magnetic field

$$
B = 0.5\,\mathrm{T}
$$

at speed

$$
v = 10^6\,\mathrm{m/s}
$$

perpendicular to the field. Determine the magnitude of the Lorentz force.

## Theory

The magnetic part of the Lorentz force has magnitude

$$
F = qvB \sin\theta
$$

If the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field, then

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

and

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

Therefore,

$$
F = qvB
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the given values:

$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19})(10^6)(0.5)
$$

First multiply the last two terms:

$$
10^6 \cdot 0.5 = 5 \times 10^5
$$

Then

$$
F = 2 \times 10^{-19} \cdot 5 \times 10^5
$$

$$
F = 10 \times 10^{-14}
$$

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Final Result

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Interpretation

The force is perpendicular to both the velocity and the magnetic field. Since it does no work, it changes only the direction of motion, not the speed.
