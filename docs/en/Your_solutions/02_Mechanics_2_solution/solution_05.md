# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision of a Runner and a Cart

## Problem Statement

A $70 \text{ kg}$ runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a $140 \text{ kg}$ stationary cart.

Determine:

1. The final speed of the cart and runner together.
2. Whether kinetic energy is conserved.

## Theory

For a perfectly inelastic collision, the bodies move together after impact. Linear momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Kinetic energy is generally not conserved in an inelastic collision.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Part 1: Final Speed

The runner has

$$
m_1 = 70 \text{ kg}, \quad v_1 = 3 \text{ m/s}
$$

The cart has

$$
m_2 = 140 \text{ kg}, \quad v_2 = 0
$$

Using conservation of momentum,

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$,

$$
70 \cdot 3 = (70 + 140)v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

Thus,

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Part 2: Check Kinetic Energy

Initial kinetic energy:

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2 v_2^2
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$,

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2
$$

$$
K_i = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \text{ J}
$$

Final kinetic energy:

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_f^2
$$

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$

$$
K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

Comparison:

$$
K_f < K_i
$$

The lost kinetic energy is

$$
\Delta K = K_f - K_i = 105 - 315 = -210 \text{ J}
$$

## Final Result

The final speed is

$$
v_f = 1.0 \text{ m/s}
$$

Kinetic energy is not conserved:

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}, \quad K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

## Interpretation

Momentum is conserved because the external horizontal impulse is negligible. Kinetic energy decreases because the collision is perfectly inelastic. Some of the initial kinetic energy is converted into internal energy, heat, sound, and deformation.
