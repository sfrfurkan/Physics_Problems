# Task 02 – Ampère's Law and Magnetic Field Between Two Parallel Wires

## Problem Statement

Two long, parallel wires are $10\ \mathrm{cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5\ \mathrm{A}$ in opposite directions. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

## Theory

The magnetic field produced by a long straight wire at a perpendicular distance $r$ is

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r}
$$

where:

- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\ \mathrm{T \cdot m/A}$ is the permeability of free space
- $I$ is the current in the wire
- $r$ is the distance from the wire

The direction of the magnetic field is determined by the right-hand rule.

When two wires carry currents in opposite directions, the magnetic fields at the midpoint between them point in the same direction, so their magnitudes add.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The distance between the wires is

$$
d = 10\ \mathrm{cm} = 0.10\ \mathrm{m}
$$

The midpoint is at distance

$$
r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.05\ \mathrm{m}
$$

The magnetic field due to one wire at the midpoint is

$$
B_1 = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r}
$$

Substituting the values,

$$
B_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5)}{2\pi(0.05)}
$$

Simplifying,

$$
B_1 = \frac{20\pi \times 10^{-7}}{0.1\pi}
$$

$$
B_1 = 2.0 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{T}
$$

Since the currents are in opposite directions, the magnetic fields at the midpoint reinforce each other. Therefore,

$$
B_{\mathrm{total}} = B_1 + B_2 = 2B_1
$$

$$
B_{\mathrm{total}} = 2(2.0 \times 10^{-5})
$$

$$
B_{\mathrm{total}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{T}
$$

To determine the direction, assume one current is upward and the other downward. By the right-hand rule, both fields at the midpoint point into the page.

## Final Result

$$
B_{\mathrm{total}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{T}
$$

Direction: into the page.

## Interpretation

Because the wires carry equal currents in opposite directions, the fields at the midpoint do not cancel. Instead, they point in the same direction and add together. This is an important contrast with the case of equal currents flowing in the same direction, where the fields at the midpoint would cancel.
