# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has frequency $f = 440 \,\text{Hz}$. The speed of sound is $343 \,\text{m/s}$ in air and $1482 \,\text{m/s}$ in water. Determine the wavelength in each medium.

## Theory

For any wave, the relation between speed, frequency, and wavelength is

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Hence,

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

The frequency of a wave is determined by the source and does not change when the wave enters another medium. The wave speed and wavelength change with the medium.

## Step-by-Step Solution

For air:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440} \,\text{m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.7795 \,\text{m}
$$

For water:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440} \,\text{m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.3682 \,\text{m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \,\text{m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \,\text{m}
$$

## Interpretation

Because sound travels much faster in water than in air while the frequency remains the same, the wavelength in water is much larger.
