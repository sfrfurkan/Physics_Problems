# Task 04 – Relative Uncertainty

## Problem Statement

A car speedometer has a $5\%$ uncertainty. If it reads

$$
v = 60\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

find the range of the car's actual speed.

## Theory

Percentage uncertainty is related to absolute uncertainty by

$$
\Delta v = \frac{p}{100}v
$$

where $p$ is the percentage uncertainty.

The actual value lies in the interval

$$
v_{\mathrm{actual}} = v \pm \Delta v
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
v = 60\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

$$
p = 5\%
$$

The absolute uncertainty is

$$
\Delta v = \frac{5}{100}(60)
$$

$$
\Delta v = 3\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

Therefore,

$$
v_{\mathrm{actual}} = 60 \pm 3\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

The lower limit is

$$
60 - 3 = 57\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

The upper limit is

$$
60 + 3 = 63\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v_{\mathrm{actual}} = (60 \pm 3)\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

Therefore, the possible range is

$$
57\ \mathrm{km/h} \leq v_{\mathrm{actual}} \leq 63\ \mathrm{km/h}
$$

## Interpretation

A $5\%$ uncertainty means that the actual speed may differ from the speedometer reading by $3\ \mathrm{km/h}$.
