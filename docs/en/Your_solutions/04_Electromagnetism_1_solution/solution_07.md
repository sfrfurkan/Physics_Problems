# Task 07 – Cyclotron Motion of an Electron

## Problem Statement

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of $5000\,\mathrm{V}$. It then enters a region of uniform magnetic field

$$
B = 0.1\,\mathrm{T}
$$

perpendicular to its velocity. Find the radius of the circular path.

## Theory

When a particle of charge $q$ is accelerated through a potential difference $V$, the gained kinetic energy is

$$
qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Thus,

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}
$$

In a magnetic field perpendicular to the velocity, the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:

$$
qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}
$$

Therefore,

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Find the Speed

For an electron,

$$
q = e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

Using

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m_e}}
$$

Substitute $V = 5000\,\mathrm{V}$:

$$
v = \sqrt{
\frac{2(1.60 \times 10^{-19})(5000)}
{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}
}
$$

$$
v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7\,\mathrm{m/s}
$$

### Find the Radius

Now use

$$
r = \frac{m_ev}{eB}
$$

Substitute:

$$
r =
\frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.19 \times 10^7)}
{(1.60 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}
$$

or

$$
r \approx 2.38\,\mathrm{mm}
$$

## Interpretation

The electron moves in a very small circular path because its mass is tiny. Even a moderate magnetic field can strongly bend the trajectory of a light charged particle.
