# Task 04 – Magnetic Torque on a Rectangular Current Loop

## Problem Statement

A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions $10\ \mathrm{cm}$ by $5\ \mathrm{cm}$ carries a current of $2\ \mathrm{A}$. A uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.3\ \mathrm{T}$ is applied parallel to the plane of the loop. Determine the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop.

## Theory

The torque on a current loop in a uniform magnetic field is

$$
\tau = \mu B \sin\theta
$$

where:

- $\mu$ is the magnetic dipole moment
- $B$ is the magnetic field magnitude
- $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic moment vector and the magnetic field

The magnetic dipole moment of a loop is

$$
\mu = I A
$$

where $A$ is the area of the loop.

The magnetic moment vector is perpendicular to the plane of the loop. Since the magnetic field is parallel to the plane of the loop, the angle between $\mu$ and $B$ is

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

Therefore,

$$
\sin\theta = 1
$$

and the torque is maximum.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the dimensions to meters:

$$
l = 10\ \mathrm{cm} = 0.10\ \mathrm{m}
$$

$$
w = 5\ \mathrm{cm} = 0.05\ \mathrm{m}
$$

The area of the rectangle is

$$
A = lw
$$

$$
A = (0.10)(0.05) = 0.005\ \mathrm{m^2}
$$

The current is

$$
I = 2\ \mathrm{A}
$$

The magnetic dipole moment is

$$
\mu = IA = 2 \times 0.005 = 0.010\ \mathrm{A \cdot m^2}
$$

Now use the torque formula:

$$
\tau = \mu B \sin\theta
$$

$$
\tau = (0.010)(0.3)(1)
$$

$$
\tau = 0.003\ \mathrm{N \cdot m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{N \cdot m}
$$

## Interpretation

The torque is maximum because the magnetic field is parallel to the plane of the loop, which means it is perpendicular to the magnetic moment. This torque tends to rotate the loop so that its magnetic moment aligns with the field.
