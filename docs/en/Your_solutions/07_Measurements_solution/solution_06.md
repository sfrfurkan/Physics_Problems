# Task 06 – Instrument Precision

## Problem Statement

A digital thermometer reads

$$
25.4^\circ \mathrm{C}
$$

Assuming the uncertainty is half the value of the last digit, find the absolute uncertainty.

## Theory

For a digital instrument, the smallest displayed increment is usually taken as the resolution.

The reading

$$
25.4^\circ \mathrm{C}
$$

has one digit after the decimal point, so the smallest displayed increment is

$$
0.1^\circ \mathrm{C}
$$

If the uncertainty is half the value of the last digit, then

$$
\Delta T = \frac{0.1^\circ \mathrm{C}}{2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The smallest displayed increment is

$$
0.1^\circ \mathrm{C}
$$

The uncertainty is

$$
\Delta T = \frac{0.1}{2}
$$

$$
\Delta T = 0.05^\circ \mathrm{C}
$$

## Final Result

$$
T = (25.4 \pm 0.05)^\circ \mathrm{C}
$$

## Interpretation

The thermometer displays values to the nearest $0.1^\circ \mathrm{C}$. If the uncertainty is taken as half of this smallest displayed unit, the absolute uncertainty is $0.05^\circ \mathrm{C}$.
