# Task 06 – Energy Dissipation in a Bouncing Ball

## Problem Statement

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0 \text{ m}$. After each bounce, it loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy.

Determine the height reached after the second bounce.

## Theory

The mechanical energy of the ball at height $h$ is

$$
E = mgh
$$

If the ball loses $30\%$ of its energy during each bounce, then it retains $70\%$ of its energy:

$$
E_{\text{after bounce}} = 0.7 E_{\text{before bounce}}
$$

Because $E = mgh$, and $m$ and $g$ remain constant, the rebound height is reduced by the same factor:

$$
h_{\text{after bounce}} = 0.7 h_{\text{before bounce}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The initial height is

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \text{ m}
$$

### Height After the First Bounce

The ball retains $70\%$ of its mechanical energy, so

$$
h_1 = 0.7 h_0
$$

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0 = 1.4 \text{ m}
$$

### Height After the Second Bounce

Again, the ball retains $70\%$ of its energy:

$$
h_2 = 0.7 h_1
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot 1.4 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

This can also be written directly as

$$
h_2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.7^2
$$

$$
h_2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.49 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The ball rises to

$$
0.98 \text{ m}
$$

after the second bounce.

## Interpretation

Since gravitational potential energy is proportional to height, losing a fixed percentage of mechanical energy at each bounce means the maximum height decreases by the same percentage factor each time. The bounce heights therefore form a geometric sequence.
