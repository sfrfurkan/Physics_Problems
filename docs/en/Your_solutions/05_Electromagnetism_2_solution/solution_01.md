# Task 01 – Gauss's Law for a Point Charge

## Problem Statement

A point charge of $+2\ \mathrm{C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1\ \mathrm{m}$ centered at the origin.

## Theory

Gauss's law relates the electric flux through a closed surface to the total electric charge enclosed by that surface:

$$
\Phi_E = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{Q_{\mathrm{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

where:

- $\Phi_E$ is the electric flux
- $Q_{\mathrm{enc}}$ is the enclosed charge
- $\varepsilon_0 = 8.854 \times 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{F/m}$ is the vacuum permittivity

For a spherical Gaussian surface centered on a point charge, the electric field is radial and has the same magnitude everywhere on the surface. However, for flux calculation, Gauss's law gives the result directly from the enclosed charge.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The enclosed charge is

$$
Q_{\mathrm{enc}} = +2\ \mathrm{C}
$$

Using Gauss's law,

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{Q_{\mathrm{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

Substituting the numerical values,

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

This gives

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11}\ \mathrm{N \cdot m^2/C}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11}\ \mathrm{N \cdot m^2/C}
$$

## Interpretation

The electric flux is positive because the enclosed charge is positive. A positive flux means that, overall, electric field lines leave the spherical surface. The radius of the sphere does not affect the total flux, as long as the sphere encloses the charge.
