# Task 02 – Propagation of Error II

## Problem Statement

The length and width of a rectangular plate are measured as

$$
L = (15.3 \pm 0.1)\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

$$
W = (8.4 \pm 0.1)\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

Calculate the area of the plate and its uncertainty.

## Theory

The area of a rectangle is

$$
A = LW
$$

For multiplication, fractional uncertainties add:

$$
\frac{\Delta A}{A} = \frac{\Delta L}{L} + \frac{\Delta W}{W}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\Delta A = A\left(\frac{\Delta L}{L} + \frac{\Delta W}{W}\right)
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given values:

$$
L = 15.3\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

$$
W = 8.4\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

$$
\Delta L = 0.1\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

$$
\Delta W = 0.1\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

The area is

$$
A = (15.3)(8.4)
$$

$$
A = 128.52\ \mathrm{cm^2}
$$

The fractional uncertainty is

$$
\frac{\Delta A}{A} = \frac{0.1}{15.3} + \frac{0.1}{8.4}
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta A}{A} = 0.00654 + 0.01190
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta A}{A} = 0.01844
$$

The absolute uncertainty is

$$
\Delta A = 128.52(0.01844)
$$

$$
\Delta A = 2.37\ \mathrm{cm^2}
$$

Rounded appropriately,

$$
A = 128.5\ \mathrm{cm^2}
$$

$$
\Delta A = 2.4\ \mathrm{cm^2}
$$

## Final Result

$$
A = (128.5 \pm 2.4)\ \mathrm{cm^2}
$$

## Interpretation

The uncertainty in the area depends on the relative uncertainties of both the length and width. Since both measurements contribute to the final area, their fractional uncertainties are added.
