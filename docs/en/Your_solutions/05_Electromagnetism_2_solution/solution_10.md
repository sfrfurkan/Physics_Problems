# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Diamond

## Problem Statement

Determine the speed of light in diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$.

## Theory

The speed of light in a medium is related to the refractive index by

$$
n = \frac{c}{v}
$$

where:

- $n$ is the refractive index
- $c = 3.00 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m/s}$ is the speed of light in vacuum
- $v$ is the speed of light in the medium

Solving for $v$ gives

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
n = 2.42
$$

Using

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Light travels significantly more slowly in diamond than in vacuum because diamond has a high refractive index. This large index is also responsible for strong refraction and contributes to the optical brilliance of diamonds.
