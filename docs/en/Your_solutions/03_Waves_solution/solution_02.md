# Task 02 – String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string has length $L = 64 \,\text{cm} = 0.64 \,\text{m}$ and a fundamental frequency of $330 \,\text{Hz}$. Determine the wave speed on the string.

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental mode has one antinode and satisfies

$$
L = \frac{\lambda_1}{2}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda_1 = 2L
$$

The wave speed is then

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

First compute the wavelength of the fundamental:

$$
\lambda_1 = 2L = 2(0.64) = 1.28 \,\text{m}
$$

Now compute the speed:

$$
v = f \lambda_1 = 330 \times 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \,\text{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v = 422.4 \,\text{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The wave speed on the string depends on the string tension and linear mass density. The given fundamental frequency and length determine that speed uniquely.
