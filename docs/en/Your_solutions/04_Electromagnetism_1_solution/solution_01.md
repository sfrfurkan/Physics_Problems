# Task 01 – Coulomb's Law at the Center of a Square

## Problem Statement

Four point charges of $+1.0\,\mathrm{C}$ each are placed at the corners of a square of side length $1.0\,\mathrm{m}$. Determine the magnitude and direction of the electric force on a charge of $-2.0\,\mathrm{C}$ placed at the center of the square.

## Theory

For two point charges, Coulomb's law gives the force magnitude

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

where

$$
k \approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \,\mathrm{N\,m^2/C^2}
$$

The force is directed along the line joining the two charges. A negative test charge is attracted toward each positive corner charge.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The center of a square is at the same distance from all four corners.

For a square of side $a = 1.0\,\mathrm{m}$, the distance from the center to any corner is

$$
r = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1.0}{\sqrt{2}} \,\mathrm{m}
$$

Thus

$$
r^2 = \frac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{m^2}
$$

The magnitude of the force exerted by one corner charge on the central charge is

$$
F_1 = k \frac{|(+1.0)(-2.0)|}{r^2}
$$

Substituting $r^2 = 1/2$,

$$
F_1 = 8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{2}{1/2}
$$

$$
F_1 = 8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot 4
$$

$$
F_1 \approx 3.60 \times 10^{10}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

Each of these four forces points from the center toward one of the corners.

Because the four charges are arranged symmetrically, the force vectors cancel pairwise:

- the force toward the upper right corner is canceled by the force toward the lower left corner
- the force toward the upper left corner is canceled by the force toward the lower right corner

Therefore, the vector sum of all four forces is zero.

## Final Result

$$
\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = 0
$$

Magnitude:

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 0\,\mathrm{N}
$$

Direction: no direction, because the net force is zero.

## Interpretation

Although each corner charge exerts a very large attractive force on the charge at the center, the symmetry of the square makes the total force vanish. The center is therefore a point of zero net electric force for this particular charge arrangement.
