# Task 04 – Electric Force and Gravitational Force in a Hydrogen Atom

## Problem Statement

Calculate the electric force and the gravitational force between an electron and a proton in a hydrogen atom, assuming an average separation

$$
r \approx 5.3 \times 10^{-11}\,\mathrm{m}
$$

Then calculate the ratio

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g}
$$

## Theory

The electric force magnitude is

$$
F_e = k \frac{e^2}{r^2}
$$

The gravitational force magnitude is

$$
F_g = G \frac{m_e m_p}{r^2}
$$

where

$$
k = 8.99 \times 10^9\,\mathrm{N\,m^2/C^2}
$$

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\,\mathrm{N\,m^2/kg^2}
$$

$$
e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

$$
m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Electric Force

Using

$$
F_e = k \frac{e^2}{r^2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F_e = 8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{(1.60 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

Evaluate the squares:

$$
(1.60 \times 10^{-19})^2 = 2.56 \times 10^{-38}
$$

$$
(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2 = 2.809 \times 10^{-21}
$$

Thus

$$
F_e \approx 8.21 \times 10^{-8}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Gravitational Force

Using

$$
F_g = G \frac{m_e m_p}{r^2}
$$

Substitute:

$$
F_g = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \cdot \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

First compute the mass product:

$$
m_e m_p \approx 1.52 \times 10^{-57}
$$

Then

$$
F_g \approx 3.62 \times 10^{-47}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Ratio

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.21 \times 10^{-8}}{3.62 \times 10^{-47}}
$$

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}
$$

## Final Result

Electric force:

$$
F_e \approx 8.21 \times 10^{-8}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

Gravitational force:

$$
F_g \approx 3.62 \times 10^{-47}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

Ratio:

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}
$$

## Interpretation

The electric attraction between the proton and electron is enormously stronger than their gravitational attraction. At atomic scales, gravity is negligible compared with electromagnetism.
