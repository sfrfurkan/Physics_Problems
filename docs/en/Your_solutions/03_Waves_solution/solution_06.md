# Task 06 – Wave Equation Parameters

## Problem Statement

A wave is described by

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

where $x$ and $y$ are in meters and $t$ is in seconds. Determine:

1. Amplitude $A$
2. Wavelength $\lambda$
3. Frequency $f$
4. Wave speed $v$

## Theory

A standard traveling wave can be written as

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

The parameters are identified as follows:

$$
A = \text{amplitude}
$$

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

$$
v = \frac{\omega}{k} = f\lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Comparing

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

with

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

gives:

$$
A = 0.05 \,\text{m}
$$

$$
k = 2\pi \,\text{rad/m}
$$

$$
\omega = 50\pi \,\text{rad/s}
$$

### a) Amplitude

$$
A = 0.05 \,\text{m}
$$

### b) Wavelength

Using

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

we get

$$
2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

so

$$
\lambda = 1 \,\text{m}
$$

### c) Frequency

Using

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

we obtain

$$
50\pi = 2\pi f
$$

$$
f = 25 \,\text{Hz}
$$

### d) Wave speed

Using

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

gives

$$
v = 25 \times 1 = 25 \,\text{m/s}
$$

Equivalently,

$$
v = \frac{\omega}{k} = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \,\text{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
A = 0.05 \,\text{m}
$$

$$
\lambda = 1 \,\text{m}
$$

$$
f = 25 \,\text{Hz}
$$

$$
v = 25 \,\text{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The equation already contains the wave parameters in standard form. The coefficient of the sine gives the amplitude, while the coefficients of $x$ and $t$ determine the wavelength and frequency.
