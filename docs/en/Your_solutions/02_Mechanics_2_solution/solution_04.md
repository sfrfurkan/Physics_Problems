# Task 04 – Energy and Momentum in a Sliding Block Collision

## Problem Statement

A $0.5 \text{ kg}$ block slides down a frictionless track from a height of $3.0 \text{ m}$. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a $1.5 \text{ kg}$ block initially at rest.

Determine the speed of the combined mass immediately after the collision.

## Theory

This problem has two stages.

### Stage 1: Conversion of Potential Energy to Kinetic Energy

Since the track is frictionless,

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

which gives the speed just before collision:

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

### Stage 2: Perfectly Inelastic Collision

Because the blocks stick together, linear momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Stage 1: Speed of the First Block Before Collision

The first block has:

$$
m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}
$$

It starts from height

$$
h = 3.0 \text{ m}
$$

Using energy conservation,

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute $g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$:

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3.0}
$$

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{58.86}
$$

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Stage 2: Inelastic Collision

The second block is initially at rest:

$$
m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}, \quad v_2 = 0
$$

Conservation of momentum gives

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$,

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Thus,

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the combined mass immediately after the collision is

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The first block reaches the bottom with significant speed because its gravitational potential energy is fully converted into kinetic energy. During the collision, momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not, because the collision is perfectly inelastic and some energy is transformed into heat, sound, and deformation.
