# Task 10 – Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire of length $2.0\,\mathrm{m}$ carries a current of $10\,\mathrm{A}$ and is placed in a uniform magnetic field

$$
B = 0.5\,\mathrm{T}
$$

Calculate the magnetic force on the wire for the following angles between the wire and the magnetic field:

a) $90^\circ$

b) $45^\circ$

c) $0^\circ$

## Theory

The magnetic force on a straight current-carrying wire is

$$
F = I L B \sin\theta
$$

where

- $I$ is the current
- $L$ is the wire length
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength
- $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field

## Step-by-Step Solution

The common factor is

$$
ILB = (10)(2.0)(0.5) = 10
$$

So the force becomes

$$
F = 10 \sin\theta
$$

### Case a: $\theta = 90^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \sin 90^\circ = 10 \cdot 1
$$

$$
F = 10\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Case b: $\theta = 45^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \sin 45^\circ
$$

Since

$$
\sin 45^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

then

$$
F = 10 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

$$
F \approx 7.07\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Case c: $\theta = 0^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \sin 0^\circ = 10 \cdot 0
$$

$$
F = 0\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Final Result

a)

$$
F = 10\,\mathrm{N}
$$

b)

$$
F \approx 7.07\,\mathrm{N}
$$

c)

$$
F = 0\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Interpretation

The magnetic force on a wire depends only on the component of the wire perpendicular to the magnetic field. The force is maximum at $90^\circ$ and zero when the wire is parallel to the field.
