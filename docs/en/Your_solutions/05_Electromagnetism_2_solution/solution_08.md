# Task 08 – Order of the Electromagnetic Spectrum by Increasing Wavelength

## Problem Statement

List the following types of electromagnetic radiation in order of increasing wavelength:

- Infrared
- Ultraviolet
- Microwaves
- X-rays
- Radio waves
- Gamma rays

## Theory

The electromagnetic spectrum can be organized by wavelength or frequency. Since frequency and wavelength are inversely related,

$$
c = f\lambda
$$

higher frequency corresponds to shorter wavelength, and lower frequency corresponds to longer wavelength.

The general order from shortest wavelength to longest wavelength is:

$$
\text{Gamma rays} \rightarrow \text{X-rays} \rightarrow \text{Ultraviolet} \rightarrow \text{Visible} \rightarrow \text{Infrared} \rightarrow \text{Microwaves} \rightarrow \text{Radio waves}
$$

Since visible light is not included in the problem list, it is omitted from the final ordering.

## Step-by-Step Solution

From the standard electromagnetic spectrum ordering, the given radiation types arranged in increasing wavelength are:

$$
\text{Gamma rays} \rightarrow \text{X-rays} \rightarrow \text{Ultraviolet} \rightarrow \text{Infrared} \rightarrow \text{Microwaves} \rightarrow \text{Radio waves}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\text{Gamma rays} < \text{X-rays} < \text{Ultraviolet} < \text{Infrared} < \text{Microwaves} < \text{Radio waves}
$$

## Interpretation

Gamma rays have the shortest wavelengths and therefore the highest frequencies and photon energies. Radio waves have the longest wavelengths and therefore the lowest frequencies. This ordering is fundamental for understanding how electromagnetic radiation interacts with matter.
