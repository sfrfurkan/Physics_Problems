# Task 05 – Percentage Calculation

## Problem Statement

A measurement of time is recorded as

$$
t = 5.45 \pm 0.22\ \mathrm{s}
$$

Find the percentage uncertainty.

## Theory

The percentage uncertainty is calculated using

$$
\% \Delta t = \frac{\Delta t}{t} \times 100
$$

where $t$ is the measured value and $\Delta t$ is the absolute uncertainty.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
t = 5.45\ \mathrm{s}
$$

$$
\Delta t = 0.22\ \mathrm{s}
$$

Substitute into the formula:

$$
\% \Delta t = \frac{0.22}{5.45} \times 100
$$

$$
\% \Delta t = 4.04\%
$$

## Final Result

$$
\% \Delta t = 4.04\%
$$

Rounded to two significant figures,

$$
\% \Delta t \approx 4.0\%
$$

## Interpretation

The measurement has a relative uncertainty of about $4.0\%$. This means the uncertainty is approximately $4.0\%$ of the measured time value.
