# Task 07 – Wavelength, Frequency, and Visible Color

## Problem Statement

The human eye is most sensitive to light with a wavelength of about $550\ \mathrm{nm}$. Determine:

1. the corresponding color in the visible spectrum
2. the frequency of this light

## Theory

Visible light occupies only a small part of the electromagnetic spectrum. Different wavelengths correspond to different perceived colors.

The relation between wavelength and frequency is

$$
c = f\lambda
$$

where:

- $c = 3.00 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m/s}$ is the speed of light in vacuum
- $f$ is the frequency
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength

Solving for frequency gives

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Color Identification

A wavelength of approximately $550\ \mathrm{nm}$ lies in the green region of the visible spectrum.

### Frequency Calculation

Convert the wavelength to meters:

$$
\lambda = 550\ \mathrm{nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9}\ \mathrm{m}
$$

$$
\lambda = 5.50 \times 10^{-7}\ \mathrm{m}
$$

Now use

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
f = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{5.50 \times 10^{-7}}
$$

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{Hz}
$$

## Final Result

Color:

$$
\text{green}
$$

Frequency:

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{Hz}
$$

## Interpretation

Light around $550\ \mathrm{nm}$ is near the region where the human eye has maximum sensitivity under bright conditions. This is one reason green light appears especially bright to human vision compared with many other wavelengths of the same intensity.
