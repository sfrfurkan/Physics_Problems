# Task 05 – Electric Field Required to Levitate a Proton

## Problem Statement

Find the electric field strength required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity near the Earth's surface.

Given:

$$
m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

$$
e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

$$
g \approx 9.8\,\mathrm{m/s^2}
$$

## Theory

For levitation, the upward electric force must balance the downward gravitational force:

$$
F_e = F_g
$$

The electric force on a charge in an electric field is

$$
F_e = qE
$$

The weight is

$$
F_g = mg
$$

Therefore, the required field is

$$
E = \frac{mg}{q}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For a proton,

$$
q = e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

Thus

$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

Compute the numerator:

$$
mg = 1.6366 \times 10^{-26}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

Then

$$
E \approx \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{N/C}
$$

Since the proton is positively charged, the electric field must point upward so that the electric force is upward.

## Final Result

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{N/C}
$$

Direction: upward.

## Interpretation

Only a very small electric field is needed to balance the weight of a proton because the proton's mass is extremely small. This illustrates how weak gravity is for elementary particles.
