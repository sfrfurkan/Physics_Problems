# Task 10 – Light Speed Measurement

## Problem Statement

Measure the speed of light using:

- a microwave oven
- a bar of chocolate or slices of cheese
- a ruler

The microwave frequency is

$$
f = 2.45\ \mathrm{GHz}
$$

The distance between melted spots is used to determine the wavelength of the microwaves.

Calculate the speed of light and compare it with the accepted value

$$
c = 300000000\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

Find the percentage error.

## Theory

Microwaves are electromagnetic waves. Their speed is related to frequency and wavelength by

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

Inside a microwave oven, standing waves are formed. The melted spots occur at antinodes.

The distance between two neighboring melted spots is half the wavelength:

$$
d = \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = 2d
$$

The experimental speed of light is

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = f\lambda
$$

The percentage error is

$$
\% \text{ error} = \left|\frac{c_{\mathrm{exp}} - c_{\mathrm{accepted}}}{c_{\mathrm{accepted}}}\right| \times 100
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Assume the measured distance between two neighboring melted spots is

$$
d = 6.10\ \mathrm{cm}
$$

Convert this distance to meters:

$$
d = 6.10\ \mathrm{cm} = 0.0610\ \mathrm{m}
$$

The wavelength is

$$
\lambda = 2d
$$

$$
\lambda = 2(0.0610)
$$

$$
\lambda = 0.122\ \mathrm{m}
$$

The microwave frequency is

$$
f = 2.45\ \mathrm{GHz}
$$

Since

$$
1\ \mathrm{GHz} = 10^9\ \mathrm{Hz}
$$

the frequency is

$$
f = 2.45 \times 10^9\ \mathrm{Hz}
$$

The experimental speed of light is

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = f\lambda
$$

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = (2.45 \times 10^9)(0.122)
$$

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = 2.989 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

Therefore,

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = 298900000\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

The accepted value is

$$
c_{\mathrm{accepted}} = 300000000\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

The percentage error is

$$
\% \text{ error} = \left|\frac{298900000 - 300000000}{300000000}\right| \times 100
$$

$$
\% \text{ error} = 0.367\%
$$

## Final Result

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = 2.989 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

$$
c_{\mathrm{exp}} = 298900000\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

$$
\% \text{ error} = 0.367\%
$$

Rounded suitably,

$$
\% \text{ error} \approx 0.37\%
$$

## Interpretation

The measured value is very close to the accepted value of the speed of light. The main experimental uncertainty comes from measuring the distance between melted spots, because the melted regions may be wide and difficult to locate precisely.

A more accurate result can be obtained by measuring the distance across several melted spots and dividing by the number of half-wavelength intervals.
